About Me

In Fall 2018, I jumped into blockchain research when I started my Ph.D. study at the University of Delaware. At that time, blockchain technology was still mainly applied in various kinds of cryptocurrencies after Bitcoin was created for 10 years. Coincidentally, I learned that IBM just announced the Food Trust system based on a blockchain framework for improving Walmart's food supply chain management. After analyzing this case study with my advisor Dr. Mark Nejad, I started investigating blockchain as a paradigm shift for protecting data security in transdisciplinary research problems. Since then, I have been lucky to work with my collaborators and invent the future of blockchain together.

My recent research interests are (1) privacy and scalability in consensus algorithms, (2) zero-knowledge proof authentication protocols, and (3) blockchain-based architecture designs such as connected and autonomous vehicular networks, electronic health records and federated learning.
